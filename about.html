About GCI

Mission & Values

Assist the victims of disasters (natural and armed conflicts) to become self-resilient. Rebuild and rehabilitate healthcare facilities and schools destroyed during catastrophes. Provide emergency preparedness training and organize conferences on conflict resolution, peace building, and public health.

History

Incorporated in 2008, GCI has become a bridge between developed and developing countries in terms of disaster response and recovery. Since 2009, GCI has shipped medical supplies and aid to D.R. Congo, Central African Republic, Haiti, Burundi, and Liberia.

Leadership

GCI values transparency and accountability. Employees, volunteers, and members follow a strict code of ethics. We respect human rights and treat all donors, partners, and beneficiaries with dignity.

Financial Accountability

Global Crisis Interventions (GCI) is efficient, transparent, and recognized by Charity Navigator and the Better Business Bureau. 95% of resources go directly to beneficiaries. For every $1 donated, $40 in medical supplies are delivered to disaster victims.
